# Visa Cross-Border Commerce Acceptance Program — Now Available for Small Business Issuers

## Summary

Visa is pleased to announce the expansion of the Cross-Border Commerce Acceptance Program to small business issuers across 14 additional countries. This program enables small business cardholders to access preferred currency conversion rates and zero foreign transaction fees when shopping at participating merchants in eligible corridors.

## Program highlights for participating issuers

1. **Co-branded marketing toolkits** for small business cardholders — digital templates, in-branch collateral, and email campaign assets for issuer-led promotion.
2. **Preferred dynamic currency conversion** rates negotiated by Visa with the cross-border acceptance partner network.
3. **Cardholder rewards** — accelerated points multiplier (2x on cross-border purchases) funded by Visa for the first 12 months of enrollment.
4. **Issuer-side reporting dashboard** giving consolidated visibility on cross-border spend volume and category mix.
5. **Cardholder communication templates** in 12 languages for inbound onboarding.

## Eligibility for issuers

This program is open to financial institutions issuing Visa Business, Visa Business Signature, and Visa Business Infinite cards in the following newly added countries: Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Croatia, Estonia, Hungary, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Latvia, Lithuania, Peru, Philippines, Romania, and Vietnam.

Issuers already enrolled in equivalent regional programs (LAC Cross-Border Commerce 2024, EMEA Frontier 2025) are auto-enrolled and need take no action.

## Enrollment

Interested issuers may enroll through the Visa Online Issuer Services portal under "Promotional Programs → Cross-Border Commerce Acceptance." Enrollment is open from 1 March 2026 through 30 September 2026.

For program inquiries, contact your Visa Account Executive or write to crossborder-commerce@visa.com.

## Important note

This program is a **promotional and marketing initiative**. There are no rule changes, no message format changes, no required system updates, and no compliance obligations for non-participating issuers, acquirers, or merchants. Issuers electing to participate will receive a separate program implementation guide describing reporting and marketing-asset access — no transaction processing changes are required.
